================================================
     How the lidar sensor can be simulated
================================================

Given a robot with pose (x, y, theta) and SlamTec RPLIDAR-A1

samplesPerRotation = samplingFrequency / rotationalSpeed;
angularGranularity = samplesPerRotation / 360;

theta corresponds to 0 degrees, measures clockwise

1. Shoot a ray out the middle of every angular sector (based on angularGranularity)
2. Find what wall that ray intersects with 
3. Check range resolution and accuracy to perturb measurement
4. Report noisy measurement with angle (if greater than 12, throw out)
5. Repeat

Simulating Lidar Sensor
- Specify your map with line segments
- Parts of this are on chatgpt
- Be aware of 

1. For every angle, calculate the shortest distance by testing a rays intersection with every line segment (smallest t)

Modify Ro between angles to account for motion.

R(t) = Ro + RdT
S(u) = a + u * (b - a) {0 <= u <= 1}

D = (Bx - Ax)(-Rdy) - (By - Ay)(-Rdx)

U = ((Rox - Ax)(-Rdy) - (Roy - Ay)(-Rdx)) / D

T = ((Bx - Ax)(Roy - Ay) - (By - Ay)(Rox - Ax)) / D

If D == 0, lines are parallel.

If U is not between 0 and 1 (can be equal) then we didn't intersect the line during the segment.

If T is less than 0, ray didn't hit it (hit it in the past haha)

(Find smallest T!)

2. Throw out any points where T < 0.15 m and T > 12 m

3. Add noise to T based on accuracy

4. Snap to resolution (rounding)


Accuracy:

(lower case sigma) Standard deviation is based on percent multiplied by distance
